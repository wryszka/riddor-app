WHAT HAPPENED
Mrs Thornton was walking through the main atrium carrying shopping bags.
She stepped onto a section of flooring where a tile had become uneven
(approximately 15mm raised edge). Her foot caught on the raised tile and
she fell forward heavily, landing on her right side.

Mrs Thornton was unable to get up. She was in significant pain,
particularly in her right hip area. Centre security attended within
2 minutes. First aider John Keane assessed the situation and called 999.
An ambulance arrived at 14:38.

Paramedics assessed Mrs Thornton at the scene and suspected a fractured
right hip. She was taken directly from the scene to City General Hospital
by ambulance for X-ray and treatment.

INJURIES
Suspected fractured right hip (femoral neck fracture)
Bruising to right arm and shoulder
Graze to right elbow

FIRST AID GIVEN
- Kept patient comfortable and warm with blankets
- Did not attempt to move the patient
- Monitored consciousness and breathing
- Ambulance called

SCENE INFORMATION
The raised tile had been flagged in last month's maintenance walkthrough
(ref: MW-2026-Feb-17) but repair had not yet been scheduled. No warning
signs or barriers were in place at the time of the incident.

CCTV footage has been secured (Camera: ATR-07, time: 14:18-14:40)

Reported by: John Keane (Security & First Aid)
Centre Manager notified: Susan Williams (14:25)
