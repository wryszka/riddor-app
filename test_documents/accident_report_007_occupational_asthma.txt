REFERRAL REASON
Ryan was referred to Occupational Health following repeated episodes of
breathing difficulty, wheezing, and chest tightness. Symptoms have been
worsening over the past 6 months. Ryan reports symptoms are worst during
and immediately after spray sessions, and improve on days off and during
annual leave.

OCCUPATIONAL HEALTH ASSESSMENT
Date of Assessment: 24 March 2026
Assessing Physician: Dr. Helen Crawford (Consultant in Occupational Medicine)

FINDINGS
Peak flow diary (kept over 4 weeks) shows a clear work-related pattern
with reductions during working days and recovery on rest days. Spirometry
shows mild obstructive pattern (FEV1 78% predicted).

Serial peak flow analysis confirms a work-related pattern consistent
with occupational asthma.

DIAGNOSIS
Occupational Asthma - caused by exposure to isocyanate-containing
two-pack paints (HDI and MDI based hardeners) used in vehicle refinishing.

This is a PRESCRIBED DISEASE under RIDDOR 2013 (Schedule 1, Table 2).

RECOMMENDATIONS
1. RIDDOR report must be submitted - this is a reportable occupational
   disease diagnosed by a registered medical practitioner.
2. Ryan must be immediately removed from exposure to isocyanates.
   Redeployment to non-spray role recommended.
3. Refer to respiratory specialist for full lung function assessment
   and ongoing management.
4. Review COSHH assessments for all spray booth operations.
5. Health surveillance for all paint shop employees to be reviewed.
6. Consider whether local exhaust ventilation in spray booths is adequate.

EXPOSURE HISTORY
Ryan has worked in the spray booth for 11 years. He uses a supplied-air
respirator during spraying but reports that it is sometimes uncomfortable
and fit-testing was last done 2 years ago. Booth extraction was serviced
6 months ago.
