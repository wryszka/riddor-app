INJURED PERSON
Name: Sophie Chen

Role: Commis Chef

Employment Start Date: September 2024

ACCIDENT DETAILS
Date: 26 March 2026
Time: 12:30 (during lunch service)
Location: Main kitchen, hot section
Witnesses: Head Chef Marco Rossi, Sous Chef Emma Watson

DESCRIPTION
During the busy lunch service, Sophie was draining a large stockpot
(approximately 40 litres) of boiling stock. As she tilted the pot at
the drain station, the pot slipped from her grip. The boiling liquid
splashed across both her forearms and the front of her chef whites.

Sophie screamed and immediately moved to the nearest cold water tap.
Chef Rossi assisted in running cold water over the affected areas for
20 minutes. The kitchen first aider assessed the burns and called 999.

Sophie was taken by ambulance to Edinburgh Royal Infirmary Burns Unit.

INJURY ASSESSMENT
Burns to both forearms (anterior surface) and lower abdomen.
Burns Unit assessment: Mixed depth burns (superficial to deep partial
thickness). Estimated total body surface area affected: 8-9%.
Sophie has been admitted for observation and wound management.

EQUIPMENT/SUBSTANCES
- 40 litre stainless steel stockpot (no handles, rim grip only)
- Contents: beef stock at approximately 95-100°C
- Drain station has no tilt mechanism or cradle

CONTRIBUTING FACTORS (PRELIMINARY)
- No mechanical tilting aid available for large pots
- Kitchen was extremely busy (covers were 20% above normal)
- Sophie has not received specific manual handling training for large
  hot vessel drainage
- Chef whites provided do not include forearm splash protection
